Languages of the world

The site "Languages of the world" is intended for Belarusian language speakers. But English speakers also can find some useful information here.

I would like to conduct a short tour of the site. It consists of several sections:

  • Мовы Беларусі (Languages of Belarus). This section is dedicated to the languages of Belarus. The articles are in different languages. Here you will find the Belarusian language grammar in English (Introduction, Alfabet, Noun, Numeral, Pronoun), the map of the Belarusian dialects and an article about the Belarusian Creol language. You can find also some information about the Westpalesian language.
  • Праект ТБМ (Belarusian Language Society project). This part of the project is conducted under the aegis of the Belarusian Language Society and comprises works of linguists from different countries and dedicated to the Belarusian language (in different languages including English). All the materials are placed here by authority of the authors.
  • Сем'і моў (Language families). This section contains a list of language families. Click on a family name and you will find a register of the languages which belong to the family. If there is any additional information the language name is linked (just click it and you will be redirected to extra data).
  • Краіны і іх мовы (Countries and their languages). In this part one can find short description of languages by the countries they are spoken in.
  • Алфавіты (Alphabets). A list of different alfabets which exist or existed in this world and also in parallel worlds :):)
  • Мапы (Clickable maps). There are two maps for today. One is the map of Europe which redirects to the description of European countries and their languages (see also section Countries and their languages). The second one allows to have a look at European countries languages in graphic form (colour linguistic maps). Today I have maps of Spain, Portugal, France, Belgium, Netherlands, Great Britain, Ireland, Switzerland and Cyprus
  • Індэксы (Languages and dialects index). You may have a look at names of European languages in Belarusian.
  • Нашыя сябры (Our friends). Useful links to the sites about the Belarusian language (short English language description available).
  • Моўныя навіны (Linguistic news). Linguistic news from all over the world (mainly from Europe). The main section is in Belarusian, but I have a separate page with linguistic news from Belarus in English. If you are interested in spreading of any information about your language or languages of your country, send your information (in any language), please.
