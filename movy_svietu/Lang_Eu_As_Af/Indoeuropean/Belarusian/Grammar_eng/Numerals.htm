In Belarusian the numbers 1-4 are adjectival and agree with the noun they qualify in case (and gender for 1 and 2). Two, three, four are followed by the noun in plural form (if masculine, in Nom.( e.g.: два сталы ‘two tables’); if feminine and neuter, the ‘non-true’ dual forms are used (see examples in o-stems Nouns).

In turn five and six are nouns and are followed by the noun in Gen. plural, and they themselves are in the Nom./Acc. case form, in the other cases they agree with the noun they qualify.

The numbers 40, 90, 100 in all oblique cases (except Acc.) have ending –a: сарака, ста чалавек ‘of forty, one hundred men’, пры сарака, ста чалавеках ‘by forty, one hundred men’.

d)      Fractional

 Fractional numbers denote a certain part of the whole: адна другая ‘one half’, паўтара ‘one and a half’, сем цэлых пяць дзесятых ‘seven point five eight'. When Fractional numerals like 1/5 or 7/10 are declined the first part is changed as a Cardinal numeral and the second as an Ordinal one:

e)      Distributive

 Distributive numerals are expressed by па + Cardinal numerals: па тры ‘three each’.
